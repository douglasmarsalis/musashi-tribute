Miyamoto Musashi Tribute

The Sword Saint of Japan


Here`s a timeline of Miyamoto Musashi`s life:

  • 1584 (Age 0) - Miyamoto Musashi is born.
  • 1591 (Age 6-7) - Musashi is taken and raised by his uncle as a Buddhist.
  • 1596 (Age 11-12) - Musashi duels with Arima Kihei in Hirafuku, Hyogo Prefecture.
  • 1599 (Age 14-15) - Duels with a man named Tadashima Akiyama in the northern part of Hyōgo Prefecture.
  • 1600 (Age 16) - Believed to have fought in the Battle of Sekigahara (October 21) as part of the western army. Whether he actually participated in the battle is currently in doubt.
  • 1604 (Age 19-20) - Musashi has three matches with the Yoshioka clan in Kyoto. (1) Match with Yoshioka Seijuro in Yamashiro Province, outside the city at Rendai Moor (west of Mt. Funaoka, Kita-ku, Kyoto). (2) Match with Yoshioka Denshichiro outside the city. (3) Match with Yoshioka Matashichiro outside the city at the pine of Ichijō-ji. Visits Kōfuku-ji, Nara and ends up dueling with Okuzōin Dōei, the Buddhist priest trained in the style of Hōzōin-ryū.
  • 1605-1612 (Age 20-28) - Begins to travel again.
  • 1607 (Age 22-23) - Munisai (Musashi's father) passes his teachings onto Musashi. Duels with the kusarigama expert Shishido (swordsman) in the western part of Mie Prefecture.
  • 1608 (Age 23-24) - Duels Musō Gonnosuke, master of the five-foot staff in Edo.
  • 1610 (Age 25-26) - Fights Hayashi Osedo and Tsujikaze Tenma in Edo.
  • 1611 (Age 26-27) - Begins practicing zazen meditation.
  • 1612 (Age 28) - Duel with Sasaki Kojirō takes place on April 13, on Ganryujima (Ganryu or Funa Island) off the coast of Shimonoseki in which Kojiro is defeated. Briefly opens a fencing school.
  • 1614-1615 (Age 30-31 - Believed to have joined the troops of Toyotomi Hideyori in the Winter and Summer campaigns (November 8, 1614 – June 15, 1615) at Osaka Castle, but no significant contributions are documented.
  • 1615-1621 (Age 30-37) - Comes into the service of Ogasawara Tadanao in Harima Province as a construction supervisor.
  • 1621 (Age 36-37) - Duels Miyake Gunbei in Tatsuno, Hyōgo.
  • 1622 (Age 37-38) - Sets up temporary residence at the castle town of Himeji, Hyōgo.
  • 1623 (Age 38-39) - Travels to Edo. Adopts a son named Iori.
  • 1626 (Age 41-42) - Adopted son Mikinosuke commits seppuku following in the tradition of Junshi.
  • 1627 (Age 42-43) - Travels again.
  • 1628 (Age 43-44) - Meets with Yagyū Hyōgonosuke in Nagoya, Owari Province.
  • 1630 (Age 45-46) - Enters the service of Lord Hosokawa Tadatoshi.
  • 1633 (Age 48-49) - Begins to extensively practice the arts.
  • 1634 (Age 49-50) - Settles in Kokura, Fukuoka Prefecture for a short time with son Iori as a guest of Ogasawara Tadazane.
  • 1637-1638 (Age 53-54) - Serves a major role in the Shimabara Rebellion (December 17, 1637 – April 15, 1638) and is the only documented evidence that Musashi served in battle. Was knocked off his horse by a rock thrown by one of the peasants.
  • 1641 (Age 56-57) - Writes Hyoho Sanju-go.
  • 1642 (Age 57-58) - Suffers severe attacks from neuralgia.
  • 1643 (Age 58-59) - Migrates into Reigandō where he lives as a hermit.
  • 1645 (Age 61) - Finishes Go Rin No Sho/The Book of Five Rings. Dies from what is believed to be lung cancer.

Famous quotes from Miyamoto Musashi's, "The Book of Five Rings".

On The Limitless Self - “There is nothing outside of yourself that can ever enable you to get better, stronger, richer, quicker, or smarter. Everything is within. Everything exists. Seek nothing outside of yourself.”

On Attaining Wisdom - “From one thing, know ten thousand things.”

On Self-Improvement - “Today is victory over yourself of yesterday; tomorrow is your victory over lesser men.”

On Achieving Your Goals - “You must understand that there is more than one path to the top of the mountain.”

On How to Approach Reality - “Think lightly of yourself and deeply of the world.”

On Mankind's Potential - “All men are the same except for their belief in their own selves, regardless of what others may think of them.”

On the 1,000 Mile Journey - “Step by step walk the thousand-mile road.”

On Living in Denial - “Truth is not what you want it to be; it is what it is, and you must bend to its power or live a lie.”

On the Lonely Road - “The path that leads to truth is littered with the bodies of the ignorant.”

On Living Life - “Fixation is the way to death. Fluidity is the way to life.”

On Power vs. Force - “Whatever your determination or will power, it is foolish to try to change the nature of things. Things work the way they do because that is the way of things.”
